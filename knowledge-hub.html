Research Translation

How food systems become data systems — and why that transformation changes everything about how we feed the world

"The shift from analogue to data-mediated food systems is not primarily a technology story. It is a power story, a governance story, and — ultimately — a question about who gets to define what counts as knowledge in agriculture."

For most of agricultural history, knowledge about a farm lived in the farmer. In the soil texture she recognised by feel, in the timing of planting she calibrated to local rainfall patterns her grandparents had named. This knowledge was distributed, embodied, and place-specific — exactly the properties that made it functional in heterogeneous local conditions.

The datafication of agriculture — the progressive instrumentation of fields, crops, soils, weather patterns, and supply chains with sensors, satellites, and analytical platforms — represents something genuinely new: the extraction of that distributed, embodied knowledge into centralised, transferable, machine-readable form. The question that has not yet been adequately answered is whether this extraction is, on balance, a gift or a dispossession.

Key Insight
Precision agricultural data does not automatically benefit the farmers who generate it. Governance frameworks — data ownership, access rights, interoperability standards — determine whether new data infrastructure expands or concentrates agricultural agency.

Why This Hub Exists

Knowledge That
Earns Its Place

"The world does not lack information. It lacks knowledge that is calibrated for the people who must act on it — in context, under constraint, with incomplete time."

Every resource in this hub exists because it passes a single test: would a thoughtful practitioner find this genuinely useful at the moment they need it? If the answer is yes, we publish it. If not, we keep working until it is.

Browse Resources
01

Clarity Over Comprehensiveness

We would rather publish one excellent, clear explainer than three exhaustive ones that obscure more than they reveal. Every piece is edited for the moment of reading — by someone with limited time and a real decision to make — not for the moment of writing.

02

Practical Before Theoretical

Every resource in this hub is grounded in something real: a field observation, a tested framework, a translated research finding, or a validated tool. We do not publish concepts that exist only at the level of abstraction. The test is always: what would someone do differently after reading this?

03

Relevant to Implementation

There is a difference between knowledge that is relevant to a field and knowledge that is relevant to implementation within a field. We target the latter. The practitioner in the middle of a decision needs a different resource than the researcher mapping the landscape — and we design for the practitioner first.

04

Usable Without Dependency

Our knowledge resources are designed to be used independently of Rankine's programmes and partnerships. We do not gate them behind programme enrolment or use them as conversion funnels. Useful knowledge should circulate freely — if it helps you, that is enough. If it brings you back to work with us, that is a bonus.

From insight to application

Use these resources alongside active research and programme delivery to turn understanding into action.
