# Rankine Knowledge Hub implementation rules

## Source of truth for article design
- The approved visual and structural standard for Knowledge Hub resource pages is:
  - `circular-economy-for-construction(1).html`
- All other Knowledge Hub resource pages must match its level of richness, hierarchy, module quality, spacing, and editorial polish.
- Do not downgrade this approved sample.

## Shared site shell
- All Knowledge Hub resource pages must use the main site’s shared navigation/header and shared footer.
- Do not create or preserve a separate article-only nav or footer system.
- Resource pages must feel like real child pages of the main Rankine site.

## Content source of truth
- Use the expanded draft documents as the source of truth for article substance.
- Preserve the substance of each draft while formatting it into a polished web article.
- Do not invent new article substance.

## Public-facing wording rules
- Never expose internal editorial or production language publicly.
- Remove phrases such as:
  - "expanded draft"
  - "source note"
  - "source-of-truth content"
  - batch labels
  - document numbers as visible page content
  - "this page implements..."
  - "suggested visual"
  - "suggested downloadable"
- Replace such wording with clean public-facing metadata only.

## Visual rules
- Do not generate decorative circle graphics or filler abstractions.
- Use real feature-image placeholders with local paths under `/Images/knowledge/`.
- Keep placeholder image areas elegant and premium until final images are supplied.
- Do not use random stock images or external URLs.

## Format quality rules
- Explainers should feel concept-led and editorial.
- Frameworks should feel structured and systems-oriented.
- Playbooks should feel procedural and implementation-ready.
- Lab Notes should feel lighter, reflective, and sharply designed.
- All four formats must feel unified but subtly differentiated.

## Scope rules
- Do not redesign the whole website.
- Do not edit unrelated pages.
- Edit only the files necessary for the requested task.
- Prefer shared reusable styles/components over one-off duplicated code.

## Implementation rules
- When asked to match a page to the approved sample, preserve the target page’s own article substance while upgrading its layout and modules to match the sample.
- Use the live site’s shared nav/footer source of truth, not the sample page’s custom nav/footer markup.
- Keep pages responsive, accessible, and visually consistent with Rankine.
