Pioneer 01

Taiwo
Ridwan

Co-Founder · Co-Director · Rankine Innovation Lab
"Intelligence is only as valuable as the clarity with which it can be communicated and the precision with which it can be acted upon."

Taiwo Ridwan is a co-founder and co-director of Rankine Innovation Lab, where he shapes the intellectual and strategic direction of the lab's work at the intersection of applied artificial intelligence and sustainable development. His contribution to Rankine is rooted in a conviction that the most consequential applications of AI are not in autonomous systems operating at vast scale — but in the quiet, rigorous work of giving researchers, practitioners, and institutions better tools for understanding and acting on complex problems.

His approach is fundamentally applied and integrative: committed to frameworks that hold up in real contexts, tools that practitioners can use without specialist AI training, and research that is designed from the outset for translation and deployment rather than isolated publication. Taiwo's thinking at Rankine draws from systems design, applied machine learning, and the philosophy of scientific practice — oriented always toward what it means for knowledge to be genuinely useful rather than merely technically correct.

Applied AI Systems Design Innovation Strategy Knowledge Translation Research Practice Sustainability Intelligence

→ African intellectual excellence is not a regional qualifier — it is a globally relevant way of building.

Pioneer 02

Adesola Habeeb
Adegoke

Co-Founder · Co-Director · Rankine Innovation Lab
"Institutional research is strongest where intelligent systems, infrastructure, and biological innovation are designed to work together."

Adesola Habeeb Adegoke serves as Co-Founder and Co-Director of Rankine Innovation Lab, where he leads research at the intersection of artificial intelligence, sustainable infrastructure, and biotechnology (biogeotechnics). His work advances an applied research agenda focused on building robust frameworks that connect scientific insight to institutional decision-making and real-world implementation.

At Rankine, his leadership combines systems-oriented scientific thinking with translational discipline, ensuring that research outputs are methodologically rigorous, operationally relevant, and suited to complex environments. He focuses on developing integrated models and programmes that strengthen long-term capability across infrastructure resilience, intelligent systems, and biotechnology-enabled sustainability.

Areas of Focus
Artificial Intelligence Sustainable Infrastructure Biotechnology Biogeotechnics Research Leadership Translational Systems
→ Strong research programmes are built to guide real institutional choices, not just produce isolated findings.
→ AI, sustainable infrastructure, and biogeotechnics deliver the greatest value when designed as one integrated system.
→ Scientific rigour matters most when it translates into durable capability across organisations and communities.
