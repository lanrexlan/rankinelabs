The Problem with Most Projects

Most innovation projects are designed around outputs, not outcomes. They produce reports that are filed, programmes that run once, or prototypes that never reach deployment. The gap between what a project produces and what it actually changes is often enormous — and it is rarely acknowledged. At Rankine, closing that gap is the design brief.

How We Design Projects

Every Rankine initiative begins with three questions: What real problem does this address? Who needs to be different when it is over? What specifically will have changed — in capability, in practice, in a system? These questions shape the design, the implementation, and the evaluation of every project we undertake, from the smallest pilot to the longest-running partnership.

The Three-Stage Cycle

Rankine's project cycle moves through three integrated stages: structured learning (building the evidence base), applied research (testing and validating in real contexts), and systemic impact (ensuring outcomes travel beyond the project boundary). These are not sequential phases — they are concurrent orientations that run through everything we build.

What Success Looks Like

A successful Rankine project produces people who can do something they could not do before; institutions that operate differently in at least one meaningful way; and knowledge that contributes to a growing ecosystem of applied understanding — whether through a published framework, a translated research piece, or a validated programme design.

Stage 01

Learning

Building the evidence base — what do we know, what do practitioners need, and what does the research say?

Stage 02

Research

Testing, validating, and refining frameworks, tools, and programmes in real deployment contexts.

Stage 03

Impact

Ensuring outcomes travel beyond the project — through open tools, trained people, and changed institutions.

What's Coming

The Next Wave of
Rankine Work

A snapshot of initiatives in planning and early development — representing the directions in which Rankine's applied innovation work is expanding. Partners, collaborators, and funders who wish to engage with any of these areas are invited to get in touch early.

In Pilot 2025

AI Literacy for Research Institutions

A structured foundational programme helping research institutions build shared AI literacy — from understanding what ML can and cannot do, to evaluating AI tools for their specific research contexts.

In Development 2025

Circular Economy Assessment Toolkit

An open-access diagnostic and planning toolkit for organisations beginning to apply circular economy principles — structured around the Rankine Circular Design Readiness Framework currently in final validation.

Planning 2025–26

West Africa Food Systems Data Initiative

A collaborative research programme to map, synthesise, and make accessible the existing body of agricultural data for West African food systems — designed to reduce duplication and accelerate evidence-based decision-making by agricultural development actors.

Planning 2026

Environmental Intelligence for Local Governance

An initiative building data infrastructure and decision-support tools for community-level environmental monitoring — designed to make real-time ecological data actionable for local governance actors without requiring specialist data science capacity.

Horizon 2026+

Responsible AI Governance Framework

A research-led initiative to develop practical AI governance frameworks for applied research contexts — covering data ethics, algorithmic accountability, and impact assessment for organisations deploying AI in scientific and development settings.

Horizon 2026+

Science-Policy Translation Network

A network-level initiative connecting applied researchers, policy actors, and knowledge translation specialists to build shared infrastructure for moving scientific findings into governance and institutional action more effectively.
